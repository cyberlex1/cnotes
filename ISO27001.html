Threat Modeling AND ISO 27001
Step Description Example
1. Define ISMS Scope Clarify which information assets are protected. Decide whether the ISMS applies to the entire company or just a department (e.g., IT).
2. Identify Information Assets Identify all critical assets (data, systems, etc.). List assets such as customer databases, email servers, and financial data.
3. Identify Threats & Vulnerabilities Analyze potential threats and weaknesses in systems. Look for threats like hacking, natural disasters, and software vulnerabilities.
4. Assess Impact & Likelihood Evaluate the likelihood and potential impact of each threat. Assess the risk of a data breach in a database and its consequences on the business.
5. Map Existing Controls Check the current security measures and their effectiveness. Evaluate existing controls like firewalls, encryption, and access control policies.
6. Develop a Risk Treatment Plan Define actions and controls to reduce risks to an acceptable level. Implement multi-factor authentication (MFA) for sensitive systems.
7. Document Results Create reports detailing the risks, impacts, and actions. Generate a risk report that includes identified threats, risk levels, and mitigation strategies.
8. Review & Continuous Improvement Regularly review and update the threat model to adapt to new risks. Conduct annual reviews and update controls based on new emerging threats.
ISO 27001 VS ISO 27002
Aspect ISO 27001 ISO 27002
Purpose Defines requirements for implementing an ISMS. Provides guidelines and best practices for implementing controls.
Document Type Mandatory requirements (for certification). Guidelines (recommendations for implementation).
Focus Focuses on establishing an ISMS and managing security risks. Focuses on how to implement specific security controls.
Certification Organizations can get certified. No certification, acts as a guide.
Controls Defines security controls to implement. Details on how to apply the controls effectively.
Target Audience Organizations seeking certification. Organizations looking for best practice guidelines.
O que é?
International standard that defines the requirements for an Information Security Management System (ISMS).
It helps helps organizations secure their information systematically and effectively — protecting customer data, internal processes, and critical systems.

Como as organizações se certificam?
Step Description
1. Internal Preparation Implement the ISMS (policies, risk assessment, controls, training, documentation).
2. Internal Audit Perform an internal or pre-assessment audit to identify and fix issues.
3. Choose Certification Body Hire an accredited organization (e.g., BSI, TÜV, DNV, SGS).
4. Certification Audit Stage 1: Document review; Stage 2: Full audit of practices.
5. Achieve Certification Certification granted (valid for 3 years) with annual surveillance audits.
6. Recertification Full re-audit after 3 years to renew the certification.
Importância para as organizações

ISO 27001:2022 is important because it helps organizations protect their information, prevent security risks, and ensure compliance with data protection laws. It shows customers that the company cares about information security and improves trust and reputation of the company. Additionally, it helps avoid financial losses and ensures the company is constantly improving its security processes.

Como fazer 27001:2022 ?
  • Obtain Management Commitment: Get top management's support and resources. (e.g., Present a business case showing ISO 27001 benefits)
  • Define the ISMS Scope: Set the boundaries of ISMS in the organization. (e.g., Apply ISMS to the IT department only)
  • Conduct a Risk Assessment (Threat Modeling): Identify risks to information assets. (e.g., Analyze risks like data breaches in the customer database)
  • Develop a Risk Treatment Plan: Plan actions to address identified risks. (e.g., Encrypt sensitive data storage)
  • Create Security Policies and Procedures: Define security rules and practices. (e.g., Enforce a strong password policy)
  • Implement Security Controls: Apply controls to mitigate identified risks. (e.g., Install firewalls and antivirus software)
  • Conduct Training and Awareness Programs: Educate employees about security practices. (e.g., Host workshops about phishing awareness)
  • Monitor and Review the ISMS: Regularly assess ISMS effectiveness. (e.g., Review security incidents quarterly)
  • Conduct Internal Audits: Audit ISMS compliance and performance internally. (e.g., Check if data access controls are followed)
  • Perform Management Review: Management evaluates ISMS and goals alignment. (e.g., Senior management reviews audit results)
  • Obtain ISO 27001 Certification: Get certified by an external accredited body. (e.g., Schedule an audit with a certification body)

Information Security Management System (ISMS)
Set of policies, procedures, and controls that organizations use to manage and protect their information assets.
Risk-based Auditing
audit plan designed to address management’s high-priority risks. It allows internal auditors to respond to the risks and provide insights to management to solve risks timely
Tipos Risco
  • Cybersecurity Risk - Risk of data breaches or cyber-attacks targeting sensitive information.
  • Compliance Risk - Risk of failing to meet legal or regulatory requirements, leading to penalties.
  • Operational Risk - Risk of disruptions in critical operations due to system failures or shortages.
  • Financial Risk - Risk of financial misstatements, fraud, or loss of assets.
  • Reputational Risk - Risk of damage to public image or customer trust.
  • Strategic Risk - Risk of not achieving business goals due to market changes or poor decisions.
  • Human Resources Risk - Risk of employee turnover or lack of skills affecting performance.
  • Supply Chain Risk - Risk of disruptions in the supply chain, such as supplier failures or disasters.
  • Environmental Risk - Risk of non-compliance with environmental regulations or hazards.
  • Technology Risk - Risk of outdated or unreliable technology causing business interruptions.
Types of Audit Risks
  • Control Risk - The risk that security measures won't work as expected.
  • Detection Risk - The risk that a problem or breach won't be noticed in time.
  • Inherent Risk - The risk that existing controls will fail to prevent or detect issues.
Risk Assessment Report (RAR)
- A standard report that includes the outcomes of a risk assessment, detailing the findings and action plan based on the identified risks in an organization.
Risk Treatment Plan
A plan that outlines how identified risks will be treated (e.g., mitigating, accepting, transferring, or avoiding), including actions and timelines.
Steps of ISO 27001 risk assessment
  • Define the risk assessment method - Choose an appropriate approach or methodology for assessing risks (e.g., qualitative or quantitative).
  • Compile the list of information assets - Identify and list all critical information assets that need protection (e.g., data, software, systems).
  • Identify the threats and vulnerabilities - Identify potential threats (e.g., cyber-attacks) and vulnerabilities (e.g., outdated software) that could impact assets.
  • Evaluate risks - Assess the likelihood and potential impact of each identified risk.
  • Mitigate risks - Apply controls or actions to reduce or eliminate risks to an acceptable level.
  • Develop risk reports - Document the results of the risk assessment, including identified risks and mitigation plans.
  • Review, monitor, and audit - Regularly review, monitor, and audit the risk management process to ensure it remains effective and up to date.
Maturity Model
A structured framework used to evaluate and improve various aspects of an organization's processes, ensuring sustainable and reliable outcomes. It helps measure the effectiveness of systems, like ISMS, and supports continuous improvements at different levels.
Maturity Model Phases
  • Incomplete - Processes are undefined or not implemented, resulting in inconsistent and unreliable outcomes.
  • Performed - Basic processes are in place but are still not fully controlled or repeatable.
  • Managed - Processes are documented and controlled, with some level of management oversight.
  • Established - Processes are standardized and defined, with consistent results across the organization.
  • Predictable - Processes are measured and monitored, with performance being predictable and stable.
  • Optimized - Processes are continuously improved based on data and feedback, ensuring the best possible performance.
ISO 27001 classification of Information
  • Confidential Information - Information that is highly sensitive and only accessible to authorized individuals.
  • Restricted Information - Information that is limited to certain groups or departments but not as sensitive as confidential info.
  • Internal Information - Information meant for internal use within the organization, not for public release.
  • Public Information - Information that is freely available and can be shared with anyone.
Stages of an External Audit
  • Stage 1: Documentation Review - Auditor reviews policies and documents.
  • Stage 2: Certification Audit - On-site audit to check compliance with standards.
  • Stage 3: Surveillance Audit - Regular audits to ensure ongoing compliance.
  • Stage 4: Recertification Audit - Re-audit after a few years to renew certification.

Risk Management e Compliance
Term Definition
Risk Management The process of identifying, assessing, and prioritizing risks, followed by the application of resources to mitigate or control the impact of those risks.
Compliance The act of adhering to laws, regulations, policies, and internal or external standards applicable to an organization.
Security Control VS Security Policies vs Procedures
  • Security Policies: High-level guidelines that define the overall security approach. (e.g., Acceptable use policy, data protection policy)
  • Procedures: Detailed steps and instructions to follow in order to comply with policies. (e.g., Steps to follow when handling sensitive data, incident response procedures)
  • Security Controls: Specific safeguards or actions to reduce risks and protect information. (e.g., Encryption, firewalls, access controls)
